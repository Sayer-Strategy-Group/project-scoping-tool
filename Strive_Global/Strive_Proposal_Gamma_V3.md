## Executive Summary

STRIVE Global is a PE-backed (Essex Bay Capital) benefits technology company headquartered in Denver, CO with approximately 51 employees. STRIVE offers white-labeled employee benefits apps, AI-powered benefits assistants, scalable microsites, and a rewards platform (Divi) distributed through a broker channel model. ### Phase 1: CRM Foundation (Weeks 1-8)

### Phase 2: CPQ, Enrichment and Integrations (Weeks 9-18)

## The Problem

After 10 years of organic growth, STRIVE's HubSpot instance has become a bottleneck -- not an asset.

### 10+ Days Per Year Lost to Manual Reporting

Andrea spends 1+ full business day every month assembling board reports by hand. That is time not spent selling or coaching.

### Zero Contact Classification

10 years of contact records with no way to distinguish clients from prospects from brokers from partners.

### Pipeline Under-Reported

Deals are quoted manually outside the CRM. They never enter the pipeline. The board sees an incomplete picture.

### Gut-Feel Forecasting

No probability weighting, no deal health scoring, no objective pipeline reviews. Forecasting is entirely subjective.

### Reps Default to LinkedIn

CRM data is unreliable, so reps work in LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Activity is never logged. Institutional knowledge is lost.

This is not a technology problem. It is a data infrastructure problem that compounds every quarter as the team scales.

## Phase 1: CRM Foundation (Weeks 1-8)

Phase 1 delivers the highest-impact fixes first: pipeline clarity, contact classification, board dashboards, and deal health scoring. It pays for itself independently and can stand alone as a complete engagement.

### CRM Architecture and Config Review

Portal audit, user roles, permissions, team structure reconfiguration, seat/license review across 3 hubs.

### Pipeline Restructuring

Pre-pipeline separation, partner/client deal categories, stage probability mapping (5/20/40/50/60/80/90/100), Contracting stage, required fields, auto-transitions with deal age reset.

### Contact and Company Classification + Cleanup

Contact type taxonomy (client/prospect/broker/partner), AI enrichment pilot on 500 records, full database classification, normalization, deduplication, association fixes, duplicate protection.

### Deal Health Scoring and Forecasting

Deal score formula (stage + time-in-stage + activity recency), healthy/at-risk/stale classification, standardized close won/lost reason dropdowns.
