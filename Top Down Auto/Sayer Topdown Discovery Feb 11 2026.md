## Notes

### **Sales Process and CRM Needs**
- The sales process depends heavily on inbound leads with a manual, inefficient order entry and follow-up system that lacks automation and clear visibility.
- **Inbound Sales Dominance with Manual Order Entry** (10:15)  
    - Majority of sales come from inbound emails or calls, with no outbound outreach currently.  
    - Customer service reps act as inside sales, taking orders and confirming via NetSuite without CRM support.  
    - Sales conversations are not recorded, and notes are fragmented, often paper-based, causing information loss during absences.  
    - The lack of CRM leads to no tracking of quote follow-ups, wins, losses, or reasons for lost sales.
- **Need for Multiple Sales Pipelines to Handle Product Variations** (16:40)  
    - Different product teams (interiors, convertible tops, seat covers) follow distinct sales cycles with unique stages.  
    - Interiors sales require complex back-and-forth for material matching, causing long lead times of four to six weeks or more.  
    - A CRM with tailored pipelines and stages per product line is essential to manage this complexity and improve process transparency.
- **Shared Services Model and Call Routing Challenges** (20:30)  
    - Current phone system (Nextiva) is costly, inefficient, and causes long customer wait times up to an hour.  
    - Calls are routed through small brand-specific groups rather than a unified round-robin queue, leading to uneven workload distribution and customer frustration.  
    - The goal is a shared pool of inside sales reps who can cross-sell across brands with integrated CRM call logging and history visibility before answering.
- **Marketing Automation and Customer Segmentation Gaps** (44:00)  
    - Klaviyo is used for both B2C and B2B email marketing but only basic functionality is utilized.  
    - There is little customer segmentation or behavior-triggered automation, limiting targeted outreach based on purchase history or interests.  
    - A modern CRM could enrich segmentation with custom company properties (e.g., specialty or brand focus) to deliver more relevant communications and improve engagement.
### **ERP and Data Integration Challenges**
- Data fragmentation across multiple ERPs and legacy systems creates operational inefficiencies and prevents consolidated reporting or smooth sales processes.
- **Two ERPs with Incomplete SKU and Customer Data Transfer** (24:00)  
    - Active efforts to migrate historic product and customer data from an old ERP into NetSuite are stalled by a **$25,000 export fee**.  
    - The team is exploring workarounds with internal IT to extract data and consolidate it into NetSuite or a SQL Server database.  
    - Legacy paper catalogs with critical product info remain undigitized, hindering quick customer service and order processing.
- **Duplicated SKUs Across Brands with Complex Product Relationships** (37:00)  
    - Separate subsidiaries maintain distinct SKUs for the same product to preserve brand loyalty and manufacturing differences.  
    - Sales reps need tools to identify equivalent SKUs across brands and understand fitment or warranty differences.  
    - A strong year-make-model lookup tool is necessary to quickly find the right product variation for customers.
- **Data Architecture Strategy and Integration Approach** (28:00)

    - The team is considering parallel integrations versus a centralized data warehouse, balancing speed and cost with long-term reporting and automation benefits.
