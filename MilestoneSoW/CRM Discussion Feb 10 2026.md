## Notes

### **CRM Needs and Relationship Management**
- The team agreed the immediate priority is establishing a simple, efficient system to track relationship interactions across multiple regional offices without manual data entry (02:56).
- **Jason Wise and Robert Strong confirmed** the main goal is to manage contacts and conversations across offices in Atlanta, Dallas, Denver, and South Florida, focusing on who talks to whom and when (05:50).
    - The aim is to avoid overlap and step on each other’s toes by having visibility on all touchpoints.
    - They are less focused on full deal flow management at this stage, wanting to keep the process straightforward.
    - Current tools like Excel and Teams are insufficient, leading to lost or duplicated information.
    - The system must automate logging important activities while filtering out unnecessary noise to keep it manageable.
- **Billy Leigh emphasized** that a CRM should fit the business process and not force users to adapt to inflexible workflows (12:12).
    - HubSpot offers flexible customization to match Milestone’s relationship tracking needs.
    - Integration with email and calendar can automate contact creation and meeting logging to reduce manual work.
    - The system will enable team members to quickly see shared contacts and interaction history, improving collaboration.
- **Jason highlighted** challenges with off-the-shelf CRMs like Airtable and Zoho, which lacked full association capabilities across contacts and companies (15:27).
    - Existing CRMs often isolate accounts, making it hard to get a full picture of interactions within a firm.
    - Custom builds are possible but can become overly complex and costly.
    - HubSpot’s association features natively support linking contacts, companies, and deals to provide the needed 360-degree view.
### **CRM Platform Evaluation and User Experience**
- The discussion centered on comparing Airtable’s spreadsheet-style flexibility with HubSpot’s systemized approach, focusing on ease of use and automation (27:34).
- **Cameron Taggart and Kyle Harbuck noted** HubSpot is more user-friendly and less prone to errors caused by accidental edits, making it better for varied user skill levels including older staff (28:40).
    - HubSpot’s interface is designed to prevent accidental damage via permissions and settings.
    - Airtable requires more manual setup and maintenance, which can burden users with less technical expertise.
- **Billy Leigh explained** that HubSpot automates contact creation and activity tracking, requiring minimal manual data entry (29:31).
    - Emails and meetings automatically generate contacts and log activities, reducing the need for user input.
    - Leads and deals can be tracked through defined pipelines, providing visibility into qualification stages and deal progress.
    - Executives can monitor metrics like meetings per week, new contacts, and deal statuses without extra meetings or manual reports.
- **Jason and Robert stressed** the importance of balancing automation with control to avoid cluttering the CRM with irrelevant details (24:52, 25:41).
    - They want to avoid capturing every minor interaction but still ensure key conversations are logged.
    - Filtering and summarization features are necessary to highlight only meaningful updates.
    - The CRM should also allow manual note entries when needed for flexibility.
### **Implementation Approach and Phased Rollout**

- **Kyle Harbuck recommended** beginning with automated activity capture and contact management to ensure adoption and user comfort (37:07).

    - Once users live in the system, additional functionalities like deal pipelines can be introduced gradually.
    - Mobile access and simple user experience are critical for field teams.
- **Cameron Taggart suggested** a discovery session to map out Milestone’s investment process and roles to tailor the CRM setup (48:44).
    - Understanding who manages which relationships and deal stages will inform configuration.
    - This session will clarify which contacts, companies, and deal stages to track.
    - Defining key properties and workflows upfront will streamline user adoption and reporting.
- **Billy Leigh offered** free upfront strategy and scoping services, charging only for implementation, positioning the project as a small, manageable effort for their team (31:38).
    - They will help clean and de-duplicate existing contact data to ensure a clean import.
    - Initial imports will be small batches to verify data quality before full migration.
    - Setting guardrails in HubSpot will prevent duplicates and maintain data integrity.
