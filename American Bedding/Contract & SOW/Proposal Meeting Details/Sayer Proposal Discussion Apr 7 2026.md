## Notes

### **CPQ Implementation and Integration Strategy**
- The team agreed on a phased approach to automate and streamline the quoting process using HubSpot integrated with NetSuite and Cubics.
- **Phased rollout with clear timelines and scope** centered on delivering a quoting engine integrated into HubSpot within **12 weeks** and an extended custom platform in **13 to 18 weeks** for about **$30,000** plus an additional optional cost for the storefront and analytics (05:06)
    - Phase one focuses on building the foundational integration to automate quoting within HubSpot using existing tools.
    - Phase two expands with a custom HubSpot app that consolidates quoting, weight calculation, and syncing with NetSuite and Cubics.
    - The timeline depends on product complexity and team availability, with faster delivery possible if conditions allow.
    - Michael Taylor and Kyle Harbuck confirmed the phased plan fits current needs and budget considerations.
- **Core benefits emphasized by Cameron Taggart and Christian Stilson** include reducing manual quote preparation time from **up to 1.5 hours per quote** to under **10 minutes**, improving sales team productivity (06:49)
    - Automation also reduces errors and administrative overhead, freeing sales to focus on selling.
    - Christian highlighted the critical need for accurate, aligned data between HubSpot and NetSuite as the foundation of success.
    - The integration aims to create a single source of truth to avoid data mismatches and improve operational efficiency.
- **Technical risks and challenges identified** around syncing weight and freight data from Cubics, which requires clarity on data ownership and update processes (22:05)
    - The weight engine will likely be a custom-built component inside HubSpot to handle product specs and freight calculations.
    - Ensuring seamless two-way sync between HubSpot, NetSuite, and Cubics is key to prevent manual workarounds.
    - The team plans to perform a deep dive into calculators and product catalogs in the first 4 weeks to blueprint the integration.
- **Decision to build natively within HubSpot** rather than buying a third-party tool was justified by Kyle Harbuck as a scalable, maintainable approach that avoids adding another system to the stack (18:29)
    - Native build reduces complexity and ongoing maintenance since new products can be added directly into the integrated platform.
    - This approach supports long-term scalability and better data visibility for executives and sales managers.
