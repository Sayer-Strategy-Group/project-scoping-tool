**Prepared for:** Jodi Gruber, CEO -- for Essex Bay Capital Board Presentation
**Prepared by:** Kyle Harbuck, Harbuck Consulting / Sayer
**Date:** March 21, 2026

---

## The Problem

STRIVE's CRM is a liability, not an asset. After 10 years of organic growth, the HubSpot instance has become a bottleneck:

- **120+ hours per year** spent on manual board report preparation (1+ full business day per month)
- **Zero contact classification** across the entire database -- no way to distinguish clients from prospects from brokers
- **Pipeline is under-reported** because deals are quoted manually outside the CRM
- **Forecasting is gut feel** -- no probability weighting, no deal health scoring, no objective pipeline reviews
- **Reps default to LinkedIn** over the CRM because HubSpot data is unreliable
- **Board reporting** requires manual calculations for pipeline segmentation, win rates, and deal analysis

This is not a technology problem. It is a data infrastructure problem that compounds every quarter as the team scales.

## The Return: Four Pillars

### Pillar 1: Time Savings (Immediate, Quantifiable)

| Activity | Current State | Future State | Annual Savings |
|----------|--------------|--------------|----------------|
| Board report preparation | 8-10 hrs/month manual work | 15-30 min dashboard pull | **96-120 hrs/year** |
| Pipeline segmentation for reviews | 2-3 hrs/week manual calculation | Real-time dashboard with quick filters | **100-150 hrs/year** |
| Manual quote creation (Option B) | 30-60 min per quote, error-prone | 10 min CPQ-generated quote with e-sign | **84-200 hrs/year** at ~20 quotes/month |
| Commission validation | Manual spreadsheet reconciliation | CRM-based view with validation workflow (Option B) | **24-48 hrs/year** |

**Conservative Year 1 Time Savings:**
- Option A: **196-270 hours** = $29,400-$40,500 at blended internal cost ($150/hr equivalent)
- Option B: **304-518 hours** = $45,600-$77,700 at blended internal cost

These are hours Andrea and Jodi get back to spend on revenue-generating activities -- selling, coaching, and strategic planning.

### Pillar 2: Pipeline Accuracy & Revenue Visibility

STRIVE's pipeline is currently under-reported. Deals exist in manual quotes, email threads, and LinkedIn conversations that never enter the CRM. Conservative estimates:

- **If 20% of active pipeline is uncaptured today**, and the average deal is $30K, capturing just 5 additional deals in the system = **$150K+ in pipeline visibility** the board can see and track
- **Deal health scoring** (healthy / at-risk / stale) ensures the team focuses on winnable deals and removes dead weight from forecasts
- **Weighted probability by stage** (5% pre-pipeline through 100% closed-won) gives the board confidence that "$X in pipeline" means something concrete
- **Segmented reporting** -- partner-level vs. client-level -- eliminates the blended average problem where a $400K broker pack distorts metrics alongside $6K client deals

**Board impact:** The board stops asking "how's the pipeline?" and starts seeing real-time, trustworthy numbers segmented by deal type, seller, and partner. No more monthly manual assembly.

### Pillar 4: Strategic Enablement (Long-Term Value)

These returns are harder to quantify but are the highest-leverage outcomes:

**Scaling infrastructure:**
- New rep onboarding drops from weeks of tribal knowledge to days with documented CRM process, recorded training, and guided pipeline stages
- As the team hires (Jodi confirmed scaling plans), each new rep ramps faster and enters data correctly from day one

**Cross-sell and up-sell engine:**
- Parent-child broker structure enables systematic microsite-to-app expansion prospecting
- Today, 250 microsite clients from the Howden pack deal have no path to app upsell in the CRM
- Each converted client represents $6K-$30K in incremental revenue
- If 10% of pack clients convert to app deals: **$150K-$750K in expansion revenue potential**

**AI readiness:**
- Clean, classified, enriched CRM data is the prerequisite for AI-powered operations
- Jodi's stated vision of an AI internal knowledge assistant requires structured data to train on
- This engagement builds the data foundation that makes every future AI initiative possible

**Board confidence:**
- PE investors see operational maturity. Clean data and automated reporting signals the company is ready to scale
- Board meetings shift from "explain the numbers" to "here are the numbers -- let's talk strategy"
