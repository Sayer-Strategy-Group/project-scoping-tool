## Products & Solutions

### 1. White-Labeled Employee Benefits Apps
- Branded employee apps serving as one-stop shop for all benefits, tools, and company resources
- Available on iOS (since ~2015) and Android
- Employers/brokers get their own branded version

### 2. AI Benefits Assistant (Launched 2024)
- Described as the "first AI Benefit Assistant" in market
- Provides actionable insights for employees on health and wellbeing decisions
- Newer product line -- may not have clean pipeline tracking in HubSpot yet

### 3. Benefits Microsites
- Intuitive, always-available hub for all programs and resources
- Quick-access phone icon for benefits info on the go
- Scaled heavily in 2025 -- deployable across entire broker books of business
- This is the product line tied to the "microsite packs" Jodi referenced

### 4. Broker Packs (Reseller Model)
- Brokers buy blocks of business (microsites, app licenses)
- Broker either builds their own or STRIVE does it for them
- Six-figure deals ($400K+ example from Jodi)
- This is the enterprise/partner sales motion that skews pipeline metrics

### 5. Divi (Rewards Platform)
- **No public information found connecting Divi to STRIVE**
- Likely an internal product name, recent acquisition, or the rewards/redemption engine not marketed externally
- Essex Bay's stated strategy included acquiring recognition/rewards businesses -- Divi may be a result of that
- STRIVE does offer gamification and rewards: points, gift cards, PTO, raffles, challenges, peer recognition with a custom catalog
- **Ask about this on the call** -- understanding how Divi fits into the sales motion and HubSpot is important for scoping

## Key Implications for HubSpot Scoping

1. **Multiple product lines in one CRM** -- Apps, microsites, broker packs, and potentially Divi all need distinct tracking. This is a pipeline architecture problem.

2. **Broker/reseller model creates complexity** -- Downstream accounts from broker packs need tracking. Association structures in HubSpot (company-to-company, deal-to-deal) will matter.

3. **Deal size skew breaks standard reporting** -- A $400K deal and a $6K deal in the same pipeline makes averages meaningless. Segmented reporting is not optional, it is essential.

4. **Board reporting pressure** -- Essex Bay has a board seat and weekly visibility. The CRM needs to produce board-ready reports without manual assembly.

5. **Scaling team** -- Andrea is hiring. New reps need a CRM that guides their process, not one that requires tribal knowledge to use.

6. **AI Benefits Assistant is new** -- 2024 launch means this product line likely has the least mature sales process and CRM tracking.

7. **COO discrepancy** -- Worth clarifying who "Lauren" is vs. Zach Beegal. Matters for NDA process and who controls HubSpot admin access.
