## Hourly Mechanics

- Time tracked in 15-minute increments
- Detailed time sheets provided with each invoice
- Client is billed only for actual hours worked

## Invoicing

- **Cadence:** Monthly, or per the terms of the applicable Statement of Work
- **Terms:** Net-15 from invoice date
- **Technology & Administrative Fee:** 5% applied to each invoice based on consulting fees
- **Expenses:** Pre-approved out-of-pocket expenses billed at cost with receipts
- **Licensing:** Client provides and pays for all software licenses required for the engagement

## Rate Card Review

Rates are reviewed annually with 30 days advance notice of any changes. Existing Statements of Work signed at these rates are honored through engagement completion.

## Leveraged Delivery Model

Sayer's leveraged model pairs senior strategic oversight with focused hands-on execution. Executive Leadership sets framing and calibrates findings against PE benchmarks; the Director owns day-to-day project leadership and the discovery workstream; an Associate handles hands-on discovery, data pulls, process mapping, and artifact production. **Strategy, PM, and hands-on-keyboard — respectively, and respectfully.**

### Tier Mix (Default for DD / Assessment / Scoping Engagements)

| Tier | Rate | Expected Mix | Role | Primary Work |
|------|------|--------------|------|--------------|
| Executive Leadership (EL) | $250/hr | 10–15% | Strategy | Engagement framing, benchmark calibration, exec-level findings review, phase shaping |
| Director | $225/hr | 30–35% | PM & Lead Analyst | Day-to-day project leadership, discovery workstream lead, knowledge-capture interviews, roadmap authorship |
| Associate | $150/hr | 50–55% | Hands on Keyboard | Data pulls, process mapping, interview notes, benchmark research, artifact production |

**Expected blended rate at default mix: ~$185/hr**
(Math: 12.5% × $250 + 32.5% × $225 + 55% × $150 = $186.88)

- The five-tier rate card above remains the published standing rate card; engagement-specific proposals surface the tier mix as a separate column on the rate card page.

## Pricing Negotiation Protocol

Load this section whenever a client pushes back on a delivered proposal. Works alongside `calibration/calibration.md` in the project-scoping-tool repo.

### Step 1 — Internal BATNA before any call

Before responding to a client pricing challenge, align internally (Kyle + Cameron + Billy) on:

| Question | Answer to lock before call |
|---|---|
| Rate floor | Minimum acceptable rate for this engagement (default: $150/hr Associate rate) |
| Scope trim list | Which workstreams can be reduced or deferred? (Training/Enablement first; architecture/audit last) |
| Client classification | Is this a "good logo"? (brand value, campfire/expansion potential, referral network) |

### Step 2 — Get off email

One reply email only. Acknowledge → signal flexibility → propose 15-min call with Billy, Cameron, and Kyle. No numbers in the email. Price negotiation happens on a call, not in a thread.

**Template framing:**
> "Thanks for the transparency — we want to get this moving for you and the team. Rather than go back and forth on this, I'd love to get 15 minutes with you, Cameron, and Billy Leigh this week. [availability ask]"

### Step 3 — The two levers (in order)

1. **Scope trim first.** Training/Enablement is the default first cut (deferrable, addable as Phase 2). Data QA / foundational architecture workstreams defend against Sayer delivery liability — protect these.
2. **Rate cut last.** Frame as a relationship investment, not a correction. Never let a rate cut imply the proposal was overpriced. Billy's stated floor: $150/hr (Associate rate).

### Step 4 — Sandbox ≠ production (recurring objection)

When a client self-builds integrations and says the price should drop proportionally:

> "Connecting and validating in a sandbox is different work from a production go-live. Data QA and pre-production audit hours protect against sandbox→production failure — those don't track 1:1 with integration build hours removed. Any issues that surface in production without an audit become a Sayer liability."

### Step 5 — Billy's close pattern

When the client is a good logo and price is the only sticking point, Billy gets on the call as the closing voice. Core values: *"Win the work, not the relationship" / "Zero In > Move Fast > Win Big."* First-round relationship discount is a calculated bet on future work (campfire implementation, referrals, logo use) — not a margin concession.

### Reference

First codified during a VoIP / property-management negotiation. $28,700 proposal → client targets ~$25K → Billy calls it a good logo play → floor ~$26,400 at $150/hr. Protocol used: internal BATNA + email to schedule + 15-min close call.
