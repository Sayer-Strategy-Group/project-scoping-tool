## Notes

### **CRM Functional Requirements**
- The team agreed the CRM must centralize and automate capturing all business development communications to build a valuable relationship database.
- **Core CRM use case defined as comprehensive conversation capture and sharing across the team** (01:22)  
    - The CRM should record all forms of communication including emails, meetings, video calls with automatic transcription, and notes from in-person talks.  
    - Jim Duey emphasized capturing conversations with brokers, competitors, and lenders to maintain a rich, accessible history.  
    - Cameron Taggart described how automatic transcription and follow-up reminders help maintain relationship depth beyond transactional interactions.  
    - This approach supports sales driven by networking and consistent engagement rather than purely transactional contacts.
- **Automation and minimal manual input are critical to adoption and data quality** (07:35)  
    - Cameron stressed that capturing contacts automatically from calls or emails reduces manual data entry and improves hygiene.  
    - The system integrates with calendar invites and emails to auto-add contacts and meetings to the CRM with minimal user effort.  
    - Effective change management will be needed to ensure the team consistently uses the system and follows data entry protocols.  
    - The CRM should be customized to inherit the company’s business processes rather than forcing new workflows.
- **Filtering and organizing contacts by role and deal relevance is key to usability** (16:24)  
    - Cameron explained the importance of defining personas like evangelists, brokers, or investment partners to filter contact lists effectively.  
    - Customizable views such as spreadsheet or pipeline formats allow users to prioritize leads, opportunities, or clients based on need.  
    - This setup helps users quickly access relevant contacts and deal information without wading through irrelevant data.  
    - The system supports setting follow-up tasks linked to contacts to keep opportunities moving.
- **The CRM must link multiple counterparties and roles to a single deal for complete visibility** (27:52)  
    - Cameron showed how deals can be connected to all related parties including lawyers, brokers, lenders, and owners in a role-based structure.  
    - This linkage ensures all relevant interactions and documents are accessible in context for each deal.  
    - The system requires ongoing deal hygiene, with weekly reviews to confirm contacts and deal stages are accurate.  
    - Cameron credited this disciplined approach with enabling 130% year-over-year growth and early deal momentum in 2024.
### **Integration and Tech Stack Alignment**
- The CRM will serve as a relationship and deal tracking hub that integrates with project management and other business systems to streamline workflows.
- **CRM focuses on relationship management and deal pipeline, while project management tools handle execution** (20:30)  
    - Cameron clarified the CRM is not meant for detailed project management like Gantt charts but can track deal stages and statuses.  
    - Tools like Asana or Notion are preferred for task tracking and project execution and can integrate with the CRM.  
    - Integration allows bidirectional data flow so project updates inform deal status and vice versa.  
    - This separation ensures users work in systems tailored for their specific roles but maintain data consistency.
- **Open APIs and native integrations enable smooth data sharing between CRM and other systems** (24:26)  
    - Cameron highlighted HubSpot’s native integration capabilities and the use of APIs to connect with Asana and other tools.  
    - These integrations reduce manual handoffs and prevent data silos across business functions.  
    - The approach supports scaling from a simple initial setup to more complex workflows as needs evolve.  
    - Emphasis on starting with basic licensing and expanding capabilities over time to control costs and complexity.
- **The broader tech stack includes onboarding, HR, finance, and invoicing systems linking to CRM and project management** (23:46)  
    - Cameron described how employee onboarding tools provision access to CRM and other apps based on role.  
    - Time tracking and invoicing systems tie back to projects and clients managed in the CRM and project tools.  
    - This integrated stack supports end-to-end visibility from deal origination to project delivery and revenue recognition.  
    - The layered system approach aligns tools to their core functions for efficiency and clarity.
- **Licensing and setup should remain flexible with annual reviews to adapt to business needs** (37:52)  
    - Cameron recommended a one-year licensing model to avoid long-term lock-ins while learning system capabilities.  
    - Pricing negotiations can be timed with vendor sales quarters for better deals.  
    - The implementation process will involve refining business processes to fit the CRM and adjusting as the team gains familiarity.  
    - This phased approach reduces risk and supports continuous improvement.

    - Cameron confirmed native iOS and Android apps exist for HubSpot, enabling field staff to add notes or contacts easily.
