## Executive Summary

American Bedding is a Vesco portfolio company manufacturing mattresses for camps, missions, education institutions, dormitories, and government/military facilities. The inside sales team processes 65+ quotes per week across email (60%), phone (20%), and website forms (20-25%).

Today, creating a single quote requires navigating four separate systems: HubSpot for lead capture, NetSuite for quote generation, Excel spreadsheets for weight and freight class lookups, and Cubics for freight rate calculations. This process takes 10 minutes to over an hour per quote -- and the resulting quotes are invisible in HubSpot.

NetSuite ERP launched January 1, 2026 and HubSpot manages web capture and deal stages. The bi-directional sync between the two systems works at the opportunity level but does not transfer quote documents, line items, or pricing during the deal lifecycle.

### Option A: CPQ Essentials + Quote Visibility (Weeks 1-10)

HubSpot CPQ setup with 40-60 core SKUs, branded quote templates, deal pipeline reconfiguration, enhanced NetSuite sync for full quote visibility, basic reporting, and inside sales training.

### Option B: Full CPQ + Automation + Integration (Weeks 1-16)

Everything in Option A plus Cubics freight API integration, guided selling configurator, full SKU rationalization (700 to 40-60), advanced automation (quote-to-order bridge, payment holds), and advanced reporting.

### Why Two Options?

Option A solves the visibility problem and gets quoting into HubSpot. Option B eliminates the system juggling entirely -- automated freight, guided selling for customers who don't know product specs, and a clean product library. Both options stand alone as complete engagements with clear ROI.

Government RFP automation (35% of revenue) is explicitly excluded from both options and positioned as a future Phase 2 once CPQ confidence is established.

## The Problem

American Bedding's quoting process is spread across four systems, creating delays, errors, and invisible pipeline activity.

### 65 Quotes Per Week Across 4 Systems

Caleb handles 65+ quotes weekly. Each one requires opening HubSpot, NetSuite, an Excel spreadsheet, and Cubics -- in that order. There is no single system where a quote can be created start to finish.

### 10 Minutes to an Hour Per Quote

Simple quotes with known products take 10 minutes. Complex quotes -- custom sizes, multiple product lines, freight calculations for remote destinations -- take up to an hour. Multiply by 65 per week and the time cost is significant.

### Quotes Are Invisible in HubSpot

HubSpot syncs deal stages and close amounts with NetSuite, but quote PDFs, line items, pricing, and quote versions are not visible. Sales leadership must open NetSuite to see any quote detail. Multiple quote versions per opportunity (common for grant budgeting and customer comparisons) are completely hidden.

### 700 SKUs, 40-60 Actually Used

The NetSuite product catalog contains 700+ SKUs with significant duplication from legacy and unused variants. Inside sales regularly quotes from only 40-60 core SKUs. The bloat adds confusion and slows product selection.

### Freight Costs Looked Up Manually

Weight and freight class data lives in Excel spreadsheets on individual desktops -- not in NetSuite or HubSpot. For every quote, reps must open the correct product spreadsheet, find the weight, look up the freight class, open Cubics, enter the data, wait for a rate, and manually transcribe it back to the quote. Truckload rates sometimes require waiting until the next business day.

### Customers Don't Know What They Need

The majority of customers cannot specify mattress dimensions, foam types, cover styles, or packaging options. Inside sales must guide them through selection -- a process that adds time and creates rework when customers change their minds.

This is not a technology problem. It is a workflow integration problem that compounds with every quote.

## Option A: CPQ Essentials + Quote Visibility (Weeks 1-10)

Option A delivers the core CPQ capability and solves the #1 pain point -- quote visibility in HubSpot. It pays for itself independently and builds the foundation Option B extends.

### CPQ Architecture and Product Library

Configure HubSpot CPQ with 40-60 core SKUs organized by product family (camp mattress, dorm mattress, vinyl, innerspring). Pricing rules synced from NetSuite. Product families, descriptions, and default configurations established for fast quote assembly.

### Quote Template Design

2-3 branded templates matching the current NetSuite quote format. Includes company header/footer, itemized line items with descriptions, subtotals, manual freight line item, payment terms (prepay, net 30, net 60), and approval signatures. Five existing NetSuite PDF quotes used as design reference.

### Deal Pipeline Reconfiguration

Modify existing HubSpot pipeline to support CPQ workflow. New stages: Request for Quote, Quote Draft, Quote Sent, Quote Accepted, Sales Order Created. Required fields per stage to enforce data capture. Stage automation for quote-to-deal progression and notification triggers.

### Enhanced NetSuite Quote Sync

Extend existing bi-directional sync beyond deal stages and amounts. Quote PDFs, line items, and version history visible in HubSpot. Sales leadership can review all quote activity without opening NetSuite. Note: This workstream may require TPI partner involvement and will be technically assessed during kickoff.

### Manual Freight Workflow and SOP

Document the current Cubics + Excel freight lookup process as a standard operating procedure. Ensures consistency across the sales team while Option B automation is developed. Includes: which spreadsheet per product line, freight class lookup steps, Cubics entry process, and rate transcription.

### Inside Sales Training

1-2 virtual training sessions for Caleb and the inside sales team. Focus: CPQ quote creation workflow, quote template usage, pipeline stage management, NetSuite sync behavior. All sessions recorded for future onboarding.

### Basic Reporting and Dashboards

1 dashboard with 6-8 reports: quote velocity (time from request to sent), pipeline by stage, quote-to-close conversion rate, monthly quote volume and value, average quote value by product family.

### UAT, Go-Live and Documentation

Test scripts covering quote creation, template rendering, NetSuite sync validation, and pipeline progression. 2 UAT sessions with Caleb and Sarah-Beth. Bug fix window. Admin guide and end-user quick reference card. 2-week hypercare post go-live.

### Project Management

Kickoff meeting, weekly sync calls, status updates, milestone reviews, and scope creep prevention. ~13% of total project hours allocated to PM.

Option A Investment: $19,500

## Option B: Full CPQ + Automation + Integration (Weeks 11-16)

Option B builds on Option A's foundation to eliminate system juggling entirely. Each workstream addresses a specific workflow gap that Option A leaves manual.

### Cubics Freight API Integration

Connect Cubics TMS to HubSpot via API to auto-calculate freight costs during quote creation. Sales rep enters destination zip code; system pulls product weight from the product library, determines freight class, queries Cubics for the rate, and populates the freight line item automatically.

**Important note:** This workstream carries the highest technical uncertainty. Cameron identified "open API documentation" but no technical assessment has been completed. A 4-8 hour technical spike during Option A is recommended to validate API capabilities before committing to this scope. A change order clause will be included for overages.

### Guided Selling Interface

Customer-facing or sales-assisted decision tree that guides product selection when buyers don't know product specs. Walks through: use case (camp, dorm, institutional), mattress size, foam series, cover type, packaging preference. Outputs a recommended product configuration for the quote.

Scope: up to 4 product line configurators. May use HubSpot CMS pages or a lightweight third-party tool depending on complexity assessment during kickoff.

### Full SKU Rationalization

Workshop-driven reduction from 700 SKUs to 40-60 core. Sayer facilitates the decision process; American Bedding owns the product decisions. Includes: NetSuite SKU export and analysis, duplication identification, old-to-new SKU mapping, product library reconfiguration, and validation testing.

Mike and Don have indicated they know which SKUs are core -- the workshop formalizes and documents those decisions.

### Advanced Automation

Quote-to-order bridge: trigger NetSuite Sales Order creation from HubSpot quote acceptance. Payment hold flags: surface NetSuite AR data in HubSpot to prevent quoting to customers with overdue balances. Discount approval workflows: route non-standard pricing to Mike for approval before quote delivery. Quote expiration notifications: automated reminders for quotes approaching 30-day expiry.

### Advanced Reporting and Dashboards

2 additional dashboards and 8-10 reports: sales performance by channel (inside sales vs. government vs. web), freight cost analysis by product line and destination, SKU performance (which products are quoted most vs. least), management overview dashboard for Mike and Patrick, quote aging and revision frequency analysis.

### Additional Training

Configurator admin training for Sarah-Beth (how to add/edit product lines and decision tree paths). Advanced CPQ features for Caleb (discount workflows, approval routing, freight integration behavior). 1-2 sessions, virtual and recorded.

### Project Management

Extended PM for 14-16 week total engagement. Additional stakeholder coordination (TPI partner, Cubics vendor). Milestone reviews between Option A go-live and Option B kickoff.

Option B Investment (includes Option A): $33,000
